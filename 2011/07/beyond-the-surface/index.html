    Beyond the Surface

    July 24, 2011 by Laurian Gridinoc

    The past week’s lectures connected togheter in an unexpected way. If you would just look briefly at the names involved and their track record you would expect technical lectures.

    Chris Heilmann started the week with the new capabilities in the web browser and the open web; capabilities which although technical in nature, aim to augment how we (as users) experience the web.

    This user focus was continued in the following lecture with John Resig, on how to build a community around open source software. It was illuminating to learn how much effort you need to invest to lift off a community, of how much you have to do past a brilliant API.

    Last lecture was with Jesse James Garrett on user experience. On how to (de)construct a webapp, how to go beyond the surface deep down to the strategy level — and from there — how to build from bottom to top. (Check his freely available chapter from ‘The Elements of User Experience’)

    This is in essence about branding, and it reminded me of Oliver Reichenstein’s “The Interface of a Cheeseburger” where he explains that “brand equals interface, not surface.” (I cannot wait for his lecture this Friday.)

    In a way all the past week’s lectures were about going beyond the surface, beyond technicalities…making me change how I was approaching my idea both as technologies and UX, making me simplify a lot while being at sketch level. The next week will be instrumental in understanding the new challenges in the newsroom, and see which of those can be addressed — I’m both excited and terrified of how much they will change my initial idea.
